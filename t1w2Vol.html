Q1 Algebraic Proof

Problem: If \(\frac{x+a}{x-a} = \frac{y+b}{y-b}\), show that \(\frac{x}{a} = \frac{y}{b}\).

  • Cross-multiply the equation: $$ (x+a)(y-b) = (y+b)(x-a) $$
  • Expand both sets of brackets: $$ xy - xb + ay - ab = xy - ay + bx - ab $$
  • Cancel identical terms on both sides (\(xy\) and \(-ab\)): $$ -xb + ay = -ay + bx $$
  • Group the terms: Add \(ay\) and \(xb\) to both sides: $$ 2ay = 2bx $$
  • Divide by 2: $$ ay = bx $$
  • Rearrange to form the required ratio (divide both sides by \(ab\)): $$ \frac{ay}{ab} = \frac{bx}{ab} \implies \frac{y}{b} = \frac{x}{a} $$
$$ \therefore \frac{x}{a} = \frac{y}{b} \quad \text{(Proven)} $$

Q3 Pythagoras with Surds

Problem: Calculate \(x\). Give your answer as a simplified surd (or integer).

(a) Triangle sides: Hypotenuse \(\sqrt{30}\), Leg \(\sqrt{12}\). Find Leg \(x\).
  • Pythagoras: \(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\)
  • \(x^2 + (\sqrt{12})^2 = (\sqrt{30})^2\)
  • \(x^2 + 12 = 30\)
  • \(x^2 = 18\)
  • \(x = \sqrt{18} = \sqrt{9 \times 2}\)
\(x = 3\sqrt{2}\) cm
(b) Triangle sides: Hypotenuse \(\sqrt{300}\), Leg \(\sqrt{200}\). Find Leg \(x\).
(Note: Based on the "integer" hint, we assume \(\sqrt{300}\) is the hypotenuse)
  • \(x^2 + (\sqrt{200})^2 = (\sqrt{300})^2\)
  • \(x^2 + 200 = 300\)
  • \(x^2 = 100\)
  • \(x = \sqrt{100}\)
\(x = 10\) cm
Q4 Area of a Triangle

Problem: A triangle has an area of \(2\sqrt{6}\) cmÂ². The base is \(\sqrt{12}\) cm (from diagram context). Find height \(x\).

  • Area Formula: \(\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} = \text{Area}\)
  • Substitute values: $$ \frac{1}{2} \times \sqrt{12} \times x = 2\sqrt{6} $$
  • Simplify \(\sqrt{12}\): $$ \frac{1}{2} \times (2\sqrt{3}) \times x = 2\sqrt{6} $$ $$ \sqrt{3}x = 2\sqrt{6} $$
  • Solve for \(x\): $$ x = \frac{2\sqrt{6}}{\sqrt{3}} $$ $$ x = 2\sqrt{\frac{6}{3}} $$
$$ x = 2\sqrt{2} \text{ cm} $$
Q5 Proof of Quadratic Formula

Problem: By completing the square on \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\), prove that \(x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\).

  • Step 1: Move \(c\) to the other side and divide by \(a\). $$ ax^2 + bx = -c $$ $$ x^2 + \frac{b}{a}x = -\frac{c}{a} $$
  • Step 2: Complete the square. Add \((\frac{b}{2a})^2\) to both sides. $$ x^2 + \frac{b}{a}x + \left(\frac{b}{2a}\right)^2 = -\frac{c}{a} + \left(\frac{b}{2a}\right)^2 $$ $$ \left(x + \frac{b}{2a}\right)^2 = -\frac{c}{a} + \frac{b^2}{4a^2} $$
  • Step 3: Combine the RHS into a single fraction (common denominator \(4a^2\)). $$ \left(x + \frac{b}{2a}\right)^2 = \frac{-4ac}{4a^2} + \frac{b^2}{4a^2} $$ $$ \left(x + \frac{b}{2a}\right)^2 = \frac{b^2 - 4ac}{4a^2} $$
  • Step 4: Take the square root of both sides. $$ x + \frac{b}{2a} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{b^2 - 4ac}{4a^2}} $$ $$ x + \frac{b}{2a} = \pm \frac{\sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} $$
  • Step 5: Isolate \(x\). $$ x = -\frac{b}{2a} \pm \frac{\sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} $$
$$ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} $$
