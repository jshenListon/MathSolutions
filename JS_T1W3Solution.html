Question 1: Strategy for Forming Equations

"Go through all the questions in the slide for this week. See if you can understand how to form Algebraic equations from all the questions."

Analysis: This question is a directive to practice a skill rather than a specific problem to solve. However, the core strategy for the subsequent questions involves these steps:

  1. Define the Variable: Choose a letter (usually \(x\)) to represent the unknown quantity you are trying to find.
  2. Translate English to Math: Convert phrases like "one third of" to multiplication (\(\frac{1}{3} \times\)) and "remaining" to subtraction.
  3. Build the Equation: Set up an equality (Equation = Value) based on the final condition given in the problem (e.g., "area is 36" or "16 cherries left").
  4. Solve: Rearrange the equation to isolate \(x\).
Question 2: Wiremu's Equilateral Triangle Pen

Problem: Wiremu makes an equilateral triangle pen with area \(36 m^2\). One side is along a wall. Find the length of fencing needed for the other two sides.

Step 1: Formula for Area
An equilateral triangle has sides of length \(s\) and all angles are \(60^\circ\).
Using the sine rule for area: $$ Area = \frac{1}{2} a b \sin(C) $$ Substituting our values (\(a=s, b=s, C=60^\circ\)): $$ 36 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot s \cdot s \cdot \sin(60^\circ) $$
Step 2: Solve for side length \(s\)
We know that \(\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\). $$ 36 = \frac{1}{2} s^2 \left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \right) $$ $$ 36 = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} s^2 $$ Multiply both sides by 4: $$ 144 = \sqrt{3} s^2 $$ Divide by \(\sqrt{3}\): $$ s^2 = \frac{144}{\sqrt{3}} \approx 83.138 $$ Take the square root: $$ s = \sqrt{83.138} \approx 9.118 \text{ m} $$
Step 3: Calculate Fencing
Since one side is along a wall, he only needs to fence the other two sides. $$ \text{Fencing} = 2 \times s $$ $$ \text{Fencing} = 2 \times 9.118 = 18.236 \text{ m} $$
Answer: 18.2 m (to 1 d.p.)
Question 3: Archie's Cherries

Problem: Joe takes 1/3. Cathy takes 1/3 of the remainder. Sally takes 1/3 of that remainder. 16 cherries are left. Find the total.

Step 1: Define Variable
Let \( x \) be the total number of cherries in the box.
Step 2: Track the Remaining Amount
  • Joe's Turn: He takes \(\frac{1}{3}\). Remaining is \(\frac{2}{3}x\).
  • Cathy's Turn: She takes \(\frac{1}{3}\) of the remainder. This leaves \(\frac{2}{3}\) of the remainder.
    Remaining = \(\frac{2}{3} \times (\frac{2}{3}x) = \frac{4}{9}x\).
  • Sally's Turn: She takes \(\frac{1}{3}\) of the current remainder. This leaves \(\frac{2}{3}\) of it.
    Remaining = \(\frac{2}{3} \times (\frac{4}{9}x) = \frac{8}{27}x\).
Step 3: Form Equation and Solve
We are told 16 cherries are left. $$ \frac{8}{27}x = 16 $$ Multiply by 27: $$ 8x = 432 $$ Divide by 8: $$ x = 54 $$
Step 4: Calculate Individual Shares (Optional Check)
Joe took: \(\frac{1}{3} \text{ of } 54 = 18\). (Left: 36)
Cathy took: \(\frac{1}{3} \text{ of } 36 = 12\). (Left: 24)
Sally took: \(\frac{1}{3} \text{ of } 24 = 8\). (Left: 16) - Matches problem statement.
Answer: Joe took 18, Cathy took 12, Sally took 8. (Total was 54)
Question 4: Mi-rae's Savings

Problem: Mi-rae spends 1/5. Puts 1/3 of the remainder in savings. Has $32 left. Find total.

Step 1: Define Variable
Let \( w \) be the weekly money.
Step 2: Form Equation
After spending \(\frac{1}{5}\), she has \(\frac{4}{5}w\) remaining.
She saves \(\frac{1}{3}\) of the remainder, meaning she keeps \(\frac{2}{3}\) of the remainder as cash.
$$ \text{Money Left} = \frac{2}{3} \times \left( \frac{4}{5}w \right) $$ $$ 32 = \frac{8}{15}w $$
Step 3: Solve
$$ 32 = \frac{8}{15}w $$ Multiply by 15: $$ 480 = 8w $$ Divide by 8: $$ w = 60 $$
Answer: She had $60 at first.
Question 5: Farmer Gerard's Pens

Context: Three pens A, B, and C. A is 15x10. B is below A (height \(x\)). C is right of B (width 11). See diagram logic derived from problem description.

(a) Area of Pen A
Dimensions are given as \(15\text{m} \times 10\text{m}\).
$$ \text{Area}_A = 15 \times 10 = 150 \text{ m}^2 $$
(b) Area of Pen B
Pen B sits directly below Pen A, so it shares the width of 15m. Its height is labelled \(x\).
$$ \text{Area}_B = 15 \times x = 15x \text{ m}^2 $$
(c) Perimeter of Pen C
Pen C is to the right of B. Its width is labelled 11m. Assuming standard rectalinear alignment with B, its height is also \(x\).
$$ \text{Perimeter}_C = 2 \times (\text{width} + \text{height}) $$ $$ \text{Perimeter}_C = 2(11 + x) = 22 + 2x \text{ m} $$
(d) Total Exterior Perimeter Expression
Let's trace the outside boundary of the combined shape (A on top of B, C to the right of B):
  • Top edge (A): 15
  • Right edge (A): 10
  • Top edge (C): 11
  • Right edge (C): \(x\)
  • Bottom edge (C): 11
  • Bottom edge (B): 15
  • Left edge (B + A): \(x + 10\)
Summing these up: $$ P = 15 + 10 + 11 + x + 11 + 15 + (x + 10) $$ $$ P = 15 + 10 + 11 + x + 11 + 15 + x + 10 $$ $$ P = 72 + 2x $$
(e) Solve for x given Perimeter = 90
$$ 72 + 2x = 90 $$ Subtract 72 from both sides: $$ 2x = 18 $$ Divide by 2: $$ x = 9 $$
Answer: x = 9
