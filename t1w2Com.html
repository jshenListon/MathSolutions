Surds & Quadratic Formula Answer Sheet
Q1 Simplifying Algebraic Surds

Problem: Write \(\displaystyle \frac{\sqrt{2p}}{\sqrt{2p} - \sqrt{p}}\) in the form \(a + \sqrt{a}\), where \(p\) and \(a\) are real constants.

  • Factor the denominator:
    Both terms in the denominator contain \(\sqrt{p}\). Factor it out:
    $$ \sqrt{2p} - \sqrt{p} = \sqrt{2}\sqrt{p} - \sqrt{p} = \sqrt{p}(\sqrt{2} - 1) $$
  • Simplify the fraction:
    Substitute the factored denominator back into the fraction. The numerator \(\sqrt{2p}\) becomes \(\sqrt{2}\sqrt{p}\).
    $$ \frac{\sqrt{2}\sqrt{p}}{\sqrt{p}(\sqrt{2} - 1)} $$ Cancel the common factor \(\sqrt{p}\): $$ \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{2} - 1} $$
  • Rationalize the denominator:
    Multiply the numerator and denominator by the conjugate \((\sqrt{2} + 1)\):
    $$ \frac{\sqrt{2}(\sqrt{2} + 1)}{(\sqrt{2} - 1)(\sqrt{2} + 1)} $$
  • Expand and simplify:
    Numerator: \(\sqrt{2} \cdot \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{2} \cdot 1 = 2 + \sqrt{2}\)
    Denominator: \((\sqrt{2})^2 - 1^2 = 2 - 1 = 1\)
    $$ \frac{2 + \sqrt{2}}{1} = 2 + \sqrt{2} $$
Final Answer $$ 2 + \sqrt{2} $$
(Therefore \(a = 2\))
Q2 Expanding Surds

Problem: Write \((5 - 2\sqrt{p})^2\) in the form \(a + bp + c\sqrt{p}\) where \(a, b,\) and \(c\) are integers.

  • Expand the bracket:
    Use the perfect square rule \((x - y)^2 = x^2 - 2xy + y^2\):
    $$ (5 - 2\sqrt{p})(5 - 2\sqrt{p}) $$ $$ = 5^2 - 2(5)(2\sqrt{p}) + (2\sqrt{p})^2 $$
  • Calculate individual terms:
    First term: \(5^2 = 25\)
    Middle term: \(-2 \times 5 \times 2\sqrt{p} = -20\sqrt{p}\)
    Last term: \((2\sqrt{p})^2 = 4p\)
  • Combine and Arrange:
    Combine terms: \(25 - 20\sqrt{p} + 4p\)
    Rearrange to match form \(a + bp + c\sqrt{p}\):
Final Answer $$ 25 + 4p - 20\sqrt{p} $$
(Where \(a=25, b=4, c=-20\))
Q3 Rationalizing Surds

Problem: Write \(\displaystyle \frac{12k}{1 + \sqrt{5}}\) in the form \(ak + bk\sqrt{5}\), where \(a\) and \(b\) are integers.

  • Setup Rationalization:
    Multiply by the conjugate of the denominator \((1 - \sqrt{5})\):
    $$ \frac{12k(1 - \sqrt{5})}{(1 + \sqrt{5})(1 - \sqrt{5})} $$
  • Expand Numerator and Denominator:
    Numerator: \(12k(1) - 12k(\sqrt{5}) = 12k - 12k\sqrt{5}\)
    Denominator (Difference of Squares): \(1^2 - (\sqrt{5})^2 = 1 - 5 = -4\)
  • Simplify Fraction:
    $$ \frac{12k - 12k\sqrt{5}}{-4} $$ Divide both terms in the numerator by \(-4\):
    $$ \frac{12k}{-4} - \frac{12k\sqrt{5}}{-4} $$ $$ = -3k - (-3k\sqrt{5}) $$
Final Answer $$ -3k + 3k\sqrt{5} $$
(Where \(a=-3\) and \(b=3\))
Q4 Quadratic Equation (Complex Roots)

Problem: Solve \(z^2 + 6kz + 15k^2 = 0\) for \(z\) in terms of \(k\). Give solution in form \(ak \pm \sqrt{b}ki\).

  • Identify Coefficients:
    \(a = 1\), \(b = 6k\), \(c = 15k^2\)
  • Apply Quadratic Formula:
    $$ z = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} $$ $$ z = \frac{-6k \pm \sqrt{(6k)^2 - 4(1)(15k^2)}}{2(1)} $$
  • Simplify Discriminant:
    Inside square root: \(36k^2 - 60k^2 = -24k^2\)
  • Simplify the Root (Imaginary Numbers):
    $$ \sqrt{-24k^2} = \sqrt{24} \cdot \sqrt{k^2} \cdot \sqrt{-1} $$ Simplify surd \(\sqrt{24} = \sqrt{4 \times 6} = 2\sqrt{6}\)
    Root becomes: \(2k\sqrt{6}i\)
  • Final Calculation:
    $$ z = \frac{-6k \pm 2k\sqrt{6}i}{2} $$ Divide all terms by 2: $$ z = -3k \pm k\sqrt{6}i $$
Final Answer $$ z = -3k \pm \sqrt{6}ki $$
Q5 Quadratic Equation (Surd Roots)

Problem: Solve \(x^2 - 6kx = k^2\) for \(x\). Simplify answer as far as possible.

  • Rearrange to standard form:
    \(x^2 - 6kx - k^2 = 0\)
  • Identify Coefficients:
    \(a = 1\), \(b = -6k\), \(c = -k^2\)
  • Apply Quadratic Formula:
    $$ x = \frac{-(-6k) \pm \sqrt{(-6k)^2 - 4(1)(-k^2)}}{2(1)} $$ $$ x = \frac{6k \pm \sqrt{36k^2 + 4k^2}}{2} $$
  • Simplify Discriminant:
    Inside root: \(36k^2 + 4k^2 = 40k^2\)
  • Simplify the Surd:
    $$ \sqrt{40k^2} = \sqrt{4 \cdot 10 \cdot k^2} = 2k\sqrt{10} $$
  • Final Solution:
    $$ x = \frac{6k \pm 2k\sqrt{10}}{2} $$ Divide by 2: $$ x = 3k \pm k\sqrt{10} $$
Final Answer $$ x = 3k \pm k\sqrt{10} $$
(Factored form: \(x = k(3 \pm \sqrt{10})\))
